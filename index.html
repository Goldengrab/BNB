Frequently Asked Questions for Beginners

Can I represent myself in court if I can't afford a lawyer?

Yes. This is called representing yourself "pro se". In Small Claims Court, representing yourself is actually the standard (and in some jurisdictions, lawyers are not even allowed!). Aequitas helps you build and organize the documents required to represent yourself successfully.

What is a "Retainer Fee" vs a "Contingency"?

A Retainer Fee is an upfront down payment made to a lawyer before they start working, which is drawn against as they bill hourly. A Contingency Fee means the lawyer receives no payment upfront and takes a percentage (usually 30-40%) of the money recovered. If you lose, they get nothing.

How do I know if my lawyer is doing a good job?

Trustworthy lawyers establish clear milestones, explain strategy in plain English, and provide transparent billing summaries. The Aequitas Case Workspace allows you to see exactly what phase your lawsuit is in, preventing "black box" syndrome.
